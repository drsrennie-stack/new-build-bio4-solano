Assessment details

Team-Based Learning (TBL)

One TBL each week, completed Thursday with your assigned team. Each has two graded parts: the iRAT, a 10 to 15 question individual quiz weighted 3x (30 points), and the tRAT, the same quiz taken again with your team, weighted 1x (10 points). Each TBL is worth 40 points, so the iRAT alone is 75% of your TBL grade. Your individual preparation is what determines the outcome. There are no make-ups, and no TBL scores are dropped.

Lab practical exams

Eight lab practical exams, one covering each week's content. Each is 50 minutes, 25 stations, 2 questions per station, 90 seconds per station. You identify anatomy from slides, pictures, charts, models, and prosected material. Spelling counts; bring a pen, not a pencil. Doors lock when the exam begins. No make-ups, and none is dropped.

Cumulative Lecture Final

One comprehensive lecture final at the end of the term, with equal representation of every module. It is weighted at 15% of your course grade and also serves as the safety net for your lowest TBL score, as described above. The exact date and time are posted on the Canvas course homepage.

End-of-day lab quizzes

At the end of nearly every lab session, one randomly selected student answers a single question covering that day's content, and the whole class earns the same score. Each quiz is worth 3 points. Students present earn the class score; absent students earn a zero. No lab quiz scores are dropped.

Depth of Knowledge (DOK)

Most questions are DOK 2 and DOK 3, designed to prepare you for healthcare applications rather than memorization: DOK 1 basic recall (about 10%), DOK 2 skills and concepts (40 to 50%), DOK 3 strategic thinking (40 to 50%), DOK 4 extended thinking (0 to 10%, bonus or optional).

Make-up and late work

All quizzes, exams, lab work, and assignments are due by their scheduled dates. No late work is accepted and there are no make-ups. All in-class assessments are completed in person; if you are absent, you earn a zero. The key to this policy is simple: do not miss class.

Scholar Points (up to 3.0% bonus)

Scholar Points reward proactive learning and academic competence. The 3.0% bonus has two requirements: 18 verified hours of approved on-campus tutoring across the term (2.0%, maximum 3 hours per week counted), and an average of 80% or higher across lab exams, lab quizzes, and TBL (1.0%, awarded only if the tutoring hours are also completed). You may also earn up to 25 supplemental Scholar Points from end-of-day lab challenge questions and Community Rounds.

No other extra credit will be given in this course. Scholar Points are the only path to any extra credit. Please do not ask. No exceptions.
