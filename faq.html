How am I graded? +

Grading

Four components, with no drops on any of them:

  • Team-Based Learning, 45%. Seven TBLs, 40 points each.
  • Lab practical exams, 45%. Seven lab practicals, ID-based on slides, models, charts, and cadaver.
  • Cumulative Lecture Exam, 5%. One comprehensive exam at the end of term. Also lifts your lowest TBL if it scores higher.
  • End-of-Day Lab Quizzes, 5%. Short community-accountability quizzes nearly every lab session.

What is a TBL, and how is it graded? +

TBL & lab exams

One Team-Based Learning session each week, on your team's TBL day, with your assigned team. Each TBL has two graded parts:

  • iRAT (individual readiness assessment): you take it on your own. Weighted 3x, worth 30 points.
  • tRAT (team readiness assessment): the same quiz, retaken with your team. Weighted 1x, worth 10 points.

Each TBL totals 40 points. Seven TBLs across the term, all count toward your grade, none are dropped.

How does the cumulative exam help my TBL grade? +

TBL & lab exams

The Cumulative Lecture Exam at the end of term is worth 5% on its own, and it also serves as a safety net: if you score higher on the cumulative exam than your lowest TBL, that exam score lifts your lowest TBL score. This is the only built-in safety net in the course. There are no other drops or makeups for TBLs.

How many classes can I miss? +

Attendance & late work

You may miss a maximum of 3 class sessions across the term, excused or unexcused. On the third absence, I reserve the right to drop you from the course. You must be present for at least 90% of a session to be counted: arriving 20 minutes late or leaving 20 minutes early counts as an absence.

You must be present for every class meeting during week 1. If you are absent during the first week, your seat may be given to the next student on the waitlist and you may be dropped. Absences are treated uniformly; there is no excused vs. unexcused distinction.

Can I use AI in this class? +

AI & devices

For self-study: yes, and encouraged. You will be taught ethical, effective ways to use AI as a learning tool, including for tutoring yourself and building study materials.

For graded work: strict limits. You may only use generative AI on assignments specifically designated as AI assignments (the title will include "AI" or the instructions will say so). You may not use AI on exams or on any graded portion of a TBL. AI detection tools may be used. An AI or plagiarism rating above 25% on a graded assignment earns an automatic zero. If unsure, ask before you submit. See the AI Honor Contract in Canvas for full requirements.

Can I use my phone or laptop in the lab? +

AI & devices

No. The lab is strictly device-free. No phones, smart watches, AI glasses, tablets, laptops, or recording devices at any time. Devices must be silenced and kept fully out of sight, not in a pocket, lap, or on the table. No digital note-taking, photography, video, or audio recording.

This is because we work with cadaveric human donors under a legal agreement with UCSF’s body donation program. The people who donated their bodies to your education trust us to protect their privacy and dignity, and they cannot consent to being photographed or recorded. Violations may result in immediate removal from the course. Students under 18 need a parent or legal guardian signature on the policy before participating in lab.
