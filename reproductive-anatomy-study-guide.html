Part 1 · Female reproductive system

1. Organs of the female system

Female · Overview
  1. List the organs of the female reproductive system, and note which structure belongs to both the reproductive and integumentary systems. Recall
  2. Which organs are the gonads, and what is the collective name for the external genitalia? Apply
  3. The ovaries are homologous to the testes. Explain what "homologous" means here. Reason

2. Ovaries

Female · Gonads
  1. Give the shape, number, and location of the ovaries. Recall
  2. When during development do the ovaries descend to the pelvic brim? Apply
  3. Reason why paired gonads that sit on either side of the uterus fit the layout of the uterine tubes. Reason

3. Histology of the ovary

Female · Histology
  1. Name the histological parts of the ovary from superficial to deep (germinal epithelium, tunica albuginea, cortex, medulla). Recall
  2. What tissue is the germinal epithelium, and why does its name mislead about where ova actually come from? Apply
  3. Ova arise from the yolk sac and migrate to the ovaries in the embryo. Reason why that means the germinal epithelium does not make eggs. Reason

4. Uterine tubes

Female · Oviducts
  1. Where do the uterine tubes run, and within which ligament do they lie? Recall
  2. Name the regions of the uterine tube from the ovary to the uterus (infundibulum with fimbriae, ampulla, isthmus). Apply
  3. The tube is lined with ciliated cells. Reason how that suits moving the oocyte toward the uterus. Reason

5. Uterus: divisions

Female · Uterus
  1. List the functions of the uterus. Recall
  2. Name the anatomical divisions of the uterus (fundus, body, cervix) plus the isthmus, and define the internal os and external os. Apply
  3. Reason why the cervix, opening into the vagina, is the part examined in a Pap smear. Reason

6. Uterine ligaments

Female · Support
  1. Name the ligaments that hold the uterus in position (broad, uterosacral, cardinal, round). Recall
  2. Which ligament is a double fold of peritoneum on either side of the pelvic cavity, and which connects the uterus to the sacrum? Apply
  3. Reason why several ligaments, rather than one, are needed to keep the uterus positioned. Reason

7. Histology of the uterus

Female · Wall layers
  1. Name the three layers of the uterine wall from outside in (perimetrium, myometrium, endometrium) and the tissue of each. Recall
  2. Where is the myometrium thickest, and what is the rectouterine pouch (pouch of Douglas)? Apply
  3. Reason why the endometrium, not the myometrium, is the layer shed during menstruation. Reason

8. Vagina

Female · Canal
  1. Define the vagina and give its functions and location relative to the bladder and rectum. Recall
  2. Name the special features of the vagina (fornix, rugae, mucous membrane lining). Apply
  3. Reason how rugae and a fibromuscular wall suit the vagina's role in childbirth. Reason

9. Vulva

Female · External
  1. Name the components of the vulva (mons pubis, labia majora, labia minora, clitoris, vestibule, greater vestibular glands). Recall
  2. Give the male homolog of the labia majora and of the greater vestibular (Bartholin's) glands. Apply
  3. The labia majora contain hair, adipose, and both sebaceous and apocrine glands, but the labia minora do not. Reason which is homologous to the scrotum. Reason

10. Perineum

Female · Region
  1. Define the perineum and give its boundaries (pubic symphysis, ischial tuberosities, coccyx). Recall
  2. A transverse line between the ischial tuberosities divides the perineum into which two triangles, and what does each contain? Apply
  3. The perineum exists in both sexes. Reason why it is described as diamond shaped. Reason

11. Mammary glands

Female · Glands
  1. Over which muscles do the mammary glands sit, and what attaches them (suspensory ligaments of Cooper)? Recall
  2. Name the internal structures (lobes, lobules, alveoli, lactiferous ducts and sinuses, nipple, areola). Apply
  3. The function is lactation, milk production and ejection. Reason what the baby's suckling triggers. Reason

Part 2 · Male reproductive system

12. Scrotum

Male · Support
  1. Define the scrotum and name its external ridge (raphe) and internal divider (scrotal septum). Recall
  2. Name the two muscles that act on the scrotum (dartos and cremaster) and give the tissue of each. Apply
  3. In the cold, the cremaster and dartos contract. Reason how that regulates testicular temperature and why that matters for sperm. Reason

13. Testes and their coverings

Male · Gonads
  1. Give the location and development of the testes, including where they begin and how they descend. Recall
  2. Name the tissue layers that cover the testes from superficial to deep (tunica vaginalis, tunica albuginea) and what the albuginea forms inside (septa and 200 to 300 lobules). Apply
  3. Seminiferous tubules sit within each lobule. Reason what happens there and why tight coiling packs in length. Reason

14. Sertoli and Leydig cells

Male · Cells
  1. Where are Sertoli (sustentacular) cells, and what barrier do their tight junctions form? Recall
  2. Where are the interstitial (Leydig) cells and what do they secrete? Apply
  3. Reason why the blood-testis barrier is needed around developing sperm. Reason

15. Sperm and spermatogenesis

Male · Gametes
  1. Name the parts of a mature sperm cell (head with acrosome, midpiece, tail) and what each does. Recall
  2. Which cells are the sperm-forming cells in the seminiferous tubule? Apply
  3. The acrosome caps the head. Reason how its enzymes serve the sperm at fertilization. Reason

16. Ducts within the testis

Male · Duct path
  1. Trace the path of sperm from the seminiferous tubules to the epididymis (straight tubules, rete testis, efferent ducts, ductus epididymis). Recall
  2. What produces the fluid pressure that moves sperm along the seminiferous tubule lumen? Apply
  3. Reason why sperm are not yet mobile when they leave the seminiferous tubules. Reason

17. Epididymis

Male · Maturation
  1. Give the shape and location of the epididymis. Recall
  2. List its functions (sperm maturation, motility, storage, propulsion by peristalsis). Apply
  3. Maturation here takes about 14 days. Reason why storage plus maturation in one organ is efficient. Reason

18. Ductus deferens and spermatic cord

Male · Transport
  1. Trace the ductus (vas) deferens from the epididymis into the pelvic cavity, and name its dilated terminal portion (ampulla). Recall
  2. List the structures contained in the spermatic cord (ductus deferens, pampiniform plexus, testicular vessels, autonomic nerves, lymphatics, cremaster). Apply
  3. A vasectomy cuts the ductus deferens. Reason why sperm can no longer reach the urethra but testosterone still enters the blood. Reason

19. Ejaculatory ducts and urethra

Male · Duct path
  1. How is each ejaculatory duct formed (seminal vesicle duct plus ampulla of the ductus deferens), and where does it terminate? Recall
  2. Name the three regions of the male urethra in order (prostatic, membranous, spongy). Apply
  3. Reason why the male urethra is described as having a dual function. Reason

20. Accessory sex glands

Male · Glands
  1. Name the three accessory sex glands and their general function. Recall
  2. Contrast the seminal vesicles (alkaline, fructose, prostaglandins), the prostate (milky, slightly acidic), and the bulbourethral glands (alkaline mucus) by secretion. Apply
  3. Reason why alkaline secretions help sperm survive the female tract and the acidic urethra. Reason

21. Semen

Male · Fluid
  1. Define semen and name the four sources of its fluid (seminiferous tubules, seminal vesicles, prostate, bulbourethral glands). Recall
  2. Give the typical volume per ejaculation and the sperm count per mL, and the count below which a person is likely infertile. Apply
  3. Only a tiny fraction of sperm reach the oocyte. Reason why a very large sperm count is nonetheless needed. Reason

22. Penis

Male · External
  1. Give the functions of the penis and name its three components (body, glans, root). Recall
  2. Name the three columns of erectile tissue (two corpora cavernosa, one corpus spongiosum) and which one carries the urethra. Apply
  3. Two ligaments support the weight of the penis. Reason why the corpus spongiosum keeps the urethra open during erection. Reason
