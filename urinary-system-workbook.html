The kidneys 4 questions

01 DOK 1 · Recall

Where do the kidneys sit, and what makes their position unusual?

Reveal answer

The kidneys are retroperitoneal, sitting against the posterior abdominal wall between T12 and L3. They are partly protected by the lowest ribs. The right kidney sits slightly lower than the left because the liver pushes it down.

02 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the three layers of tissue surrounding each kidney, from outside in.

Reveal answer

Renal fascia (outermost, dense irregular CT anchoring the kidney to surrounding structures), adipose capsule (cushioning fat layer), and renal capsule (smooth fibrous covering directly on the kidney surface).

03 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the major gross structures inside a kidney from outside in.

Reveal answer

Cortex (outer layer), medulla (inner region containing renal pyramids), renal pyramids (cone-shaped structures of the medulla, with bases at the cortex and apices at the renal papillae), renal columns (extensions of the cortex between pyramids), minor calyces (cup-shaped drains around each papilla), major calyces (formed by joining of minor calyces), and the renal pelvis (the funnel that empties into the ureter).

04 DOK 1 · Recall

What is the renal hilum, and what passes through it?

Reveal answer

The hilum is the medial indentation of the kidney where structures enter and exit. Renal artery enters, renal vein exits, ureter exits. Nerves and lymphatics also pass here.

The nephron 4 questions

05 DOK 1 · Recall

What is a nephron, and roughly how many are in each kidney?

Reveal answer

A nephron is the functional unit of the kidney, the structure that filters blood and forms urine. Each kidney contains about a million nephrons.

06 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the major parts of a nephron in order from blood entry to where urine drains out.

Reveal answer

Glomerulus (a capillary tuft enclosed in Bowman's capsule, where filtration occurs) → proximal convoluted tubule → nephron loop (loop of Henle, with descending and ascending limbs) → distal convoluted tubule → collecting duct (which carries urine to the papilla and into a minor calyx).

07 DOK 1 · Recall

What is the renal corpuscle, and what are its two parts?

Reveal answer

The renal corpuscle is the filtering apparatus at the start of each nephron, made of the glomerulus (a tuft of capillaries) and Bowman's capsule (the cup-shaped beginning of the nephron tubule that surrounds the glomerulus). Plasma is filtered from the glomerular capillaries across the filtration membrane into the capsular space and from there into the proximal tubule.

08 DOK 2 · Compare

Compare cortical and juxtamedullary nephrons.

Reveal answer

Cortical nephrons (about 85 percent): renal corpuscle sits in the outer cortex; the loop of Henle is short and barely enters the medulla. Juxtamedullary nephrons (about 15 percent): corpuscle sits at the cortex-medulla boundary; the loop of Henle is long and reaches deep into the medulla. The long loops are essential for concentrating urine.

Ureters, bladder, urethra 5 questions

09 DOK 1 · Recall

Describe the ureters and how they connect kidneys to the bladder.

Reveal answer

The ureters are paired muscular tubes about 25 cm long that descend from the renal pelvis of each kidney along the posterior abdominal wall to the bladder. They are retroperitoneal. Smooth muscle in their walls peristalsis urine downward. They enter the bladder obliquely through the muscular wall, an arrangement that prevents backflow when the bladder fills.

10 DOK 1 · Recall

Describe the urinary bladder.

Reveal answer

The bladder is a hollow muscular organ in the pelvic cavity, behind the pubic symphysis. Its wall has three muscle layers collectively called the detrusor muscle. The trigone is a smooth triangular area on the inner posterior wall, defined by the two ureteral openings and the urethral opening. The inner surface elsewhere is folded into rugae that flatten as the bladder fills.

11 DOK 1 · Recall

What kind of epithelium lines the bladder, and why?

Reveal answer

Transitional epithelium (urothelium). Its cells can stretch and flatten when the bladder fills, then stack back up when it empties. The lining accommodates dramatic volume change without losing barrier function — exactly what a bladder needs.

12 DOK 2 · Compare

Compare the male and female urethra.

Reveal answer

Male urethra: about 18-20 cm long, three regions (prostatic, membranous, spongy), carries both urine and semen. Female urethra: about 4 cm long, urinary only, opens between the clitoris and vaginal opening. The female urethra's shortness explains the higher rate of urinary tract infections in women: bacteria have a shorter trip to the bladder.

13 DOK 1 · Recall

Compare the internal and external urethral sphincters by tissue type and control.

Reveal answer

Internal urethral sphincter: smooth muscle at the bladder-urethra junction, involuntary, autonomic. External urethral sphincter: skeletal muscle surrounding the membranous urethra in males and the mid-urethra in females, voluntary, somatic. Both must relax for urination.
