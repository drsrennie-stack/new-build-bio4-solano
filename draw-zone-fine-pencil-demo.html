Section 01

Why we draw before we Google

You will not retain what you only read. Drawing forces your motor cortex and visual cortex to negotiate with what your memory actually holds. The gap between what you think you remember and what your hand can produce is the gap you need to close before the lab exam.

Two new tools live in the toolbar below: a Fine pencil for hairline detail (capillary loops, suture lines, fascicles) and Apple Pencil pressure sensitivity, which automatically thins your line when you press lightly and thickens it when you press harder. You do not toggle pressure on or off. It just works when a stylus is detected.

Section 02

Tool guide

Color. Pick the pen color before you draw. Tap a different color mid-sketch to layer.

Width. Fine for detail. Normal for general structure. Bold for emphasis or arrows. Apple Pencil pressure scales within each width, so Fine plus light pressure is your hairline.

Eraser. Toggle the eraser, then drag over what you want gone. Tap a color again to leave eraser mode.

Undo and Clear. Undo removes the last stroke. Clear wipes the whole canvas.

Input. Pencil mode rejects palm and finger touches (iPad with Apple Pencil). All Input mode accepts everything (finger, mouse, trackpad). Default is All Input so the tool works on any device.
