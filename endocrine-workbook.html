Endocrine overview 2 questions

01 DOK 1 · Recall

Compare endocrine and exocrine glands by anatomy and how each delivers its product.

Reveal answer

Endocrine glands are ductless; they release hormones into the surrounding interstitial fluid, which enters the blood. Exocrine glands have ducts and release their product onto a surface or into a cavity. The same organ can have both functions: the pancreas, for example, has exocrine acini and endocrine islets.

02 DOK 1 · Recall

List the major endocrine glands of the body.

Reveal answer

Pituitary, pineal, thyroid, parathyroids, adrenals, pancreas (islets), gonads (testes/ovaries), thymus. Hormone-producing cells also live in many other organs (heart, kidneys, GI tract, adipose tissue), but the listed glands are the primary endocrine organs.

Pituitary and hypothalamus 4 questions

03 DOK 1 · Recall

Where does the pituitary gland sit?

Reveal answer

In the sella turcica of the sphenoid bone, hanging from the hypothalamus by the infundibulum (pituitary stalk). It sits at the base of the brain, just behind the optic chiasm.

04 DOK 2 · Compare

Compare the anterior and posterior pituitary by tissue origin and how they connect to the hypothalamus.

Reveal answer

Anterior pituitary (adenohypophysis): glandular epithelial tissue, derived from oral ectoderm; connected to the hypothalamus by a portal blood system that delivers releasing hormones. Posterior pituitary (neurohypophysis): neural tissue, an extension of the hypothalamus; stores and releases two hormones (oxytocin, ADH) made by hypothalamic neurons whose axons descend through the stalk.

05 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the major hormones of the anterior pituitary.

Reveal answer

Growth hormone (GH), thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH), adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH), follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH), luteinizing hormone (LH), and prolactin. Most of these are tropic hormones that stimulate other endocrine glands.

06 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the two hormones released by the posterior pituitary and what each does in basic terms.

Reveal answer

Antidiuretic hormone (ADH, vasopressin): tells the kidneys to retain water. Oxytocin: stimulates uterine contraction during labor and milk ejection during nursing.

Thyroid and parathyroids 3 questions

07 DOK 1 · Recall

Where is the thyroid, and what is its general shape?

Reveal answer

In the anterior neck just below the larynx, draped over the trachea. Two lobes connected by a midline isthmus. A small pyramidal lobe sometimes extends upward from the isthmus.

08 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the two cell types of the thyroid and what each produces.

Reveal answer

Follicular cells: line the colloid-filled follicles and produce the thyroid hormones T3 and T4 (which regulate metabolism). Parafollicular (C) cells: scattered between follicles and produce calcitonin (which lowers blood calcium by inhibiting osteoclasts).

09 DOK 1 · Recall

Where are the parathyroid glands, and what do they do?

Reveal answer

Four small glands (typically) embedded in the posterior surface of the thyroid lobes. They secrete parathyroid hormone (PTH), which raises blood calcium by stimulating osteoclasts, increasing renal calcium reabsorption, and indirectly increasing intestinal calcium absorption. PTH and calcitonin act in opposition on bone calcium.

Adrenal glands 2 questions

10 DOK 1 · Recall

Where do the adrenal glands sit?

Reveal answer

On top of each kidney (suprarenal glands). Right is more pyramidal; left is more crescent-shaped. They are retroperitoneal.

11 DOK 2 · Compare

Compare the adrenal cortex and adrenal medulla by tissue origin and what each secretes.

Reveal answer

Adrenal cortex (outer): derived from mesoderm; produces steroid hormones in three zones — zona glomerulosa (aldosterone, regulates sodium and potassium), zona fasciculata (cortisol, stress and metabolism), zona reticularis (adrenal androgens). Adrenal medulla (inner): derived from neural crest; modified sympathetic ganglion; secretes catecholamines (epinephrine, norepinephrine) into the blood.

Pancreas, gonads, pineal, thymus 4 questions

12 DOK 1 · Recall

What are the pancreatic islets, and what are their main cell types?

Reveal answer

Pancreatic islets (islets of Langerhans) are clusters of endocrine cells scattered through the otherwise exocrine pancreas. Alpha cells secrete glucagon (raises blood glucose). Beta cells secrete insulin (lowers blood glucose). Delta cells secrete somatostatin.

13 DOK 1 · Recall

What hormones do the testes and ovaries produce, beyond their gamete production role?

Reveal answer

Testes: testosterone (mostly from Leydig cells). Ovaries: estrogens and progesterone (from developing follicles and the corpus luteum). All three are steroid hormones.

14 DOK 1 · Recall

Where is the pineal gland, and what does it secrete?

Reveal answer

A small cone-shaped gland in the brain, posterior to the third ventricle. It secretes melatonin, which is involved in circadian rhythm and the sleep-wake cycle.

15 DOK 1 · Recall

Why is the thymus considered both a lymphoid and an endocrine organ?

Reveal answer

It is the maturation site for T lymphocytes (lymphoid role) and it also secretes thymic hormones (thymosin, thymopoietin) that influence T-cell development (endocrine role). It is most active in childhood and shrinks with age.
