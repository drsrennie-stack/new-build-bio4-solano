core Heart wall, pericardium, internal features cv-surfaces5r · 2q · 1s

cv-surfaces-r1DOK 1recall

Name the three layers of the heart wall, from outer to inner.

A. Epicardium, myocardium, endocardium.

cv-surfaces-r2DOK 1recall

What is the myocardium, and what is its job?

A. The thick middle layer of cardiac muscle, the bulk of the heart, the layer that contracts.

cv-surfaces-r3DOK 1recall

What is the pericardium?

A. The double-walled sac that encloses the heart.

cv-surfaces-r4DOK 2recall

Compare the fibrous and serous pericardium.

A. The fibrous pericardium is the tough outer layer that anchors the heart; the serous pericardium is the inner double membrane with a parietal and a visceral layer.

cv-surfaces-r5DOK 3recall

Fluid builds up in the pericardial cavity and squeezes the heart. What is this, and why does it impair filling?

A. Cardiac tamponade; the fluid compresses the heart from outside and limits how much it can fill.

cv-surfaces-q1DOK 1mcq

Which layer of the heart wall contracts?

A. Epicardium

B. Myocardium

C. Endocardium

D. Pericardium

The myocardium is the thick cardiac-muscle layer that contracts. The epicardium and endocardium are thin surface layers, and the pericardium is the surrounding sac.

cv-surfaces-q2DOK 2mcq

Excess fluid in the pericardial cavity that compresses the heart is called what?

A. Cardiac tamponade

B. Myocardial infarction

C. Endocarditis

D. Angina

Cardiac tamponade is fluid compressing the heart from outside, limiting filling. A myocardial infarction is dying muscle from a blocked artery.

cv-surfaces-s1DOK 3stretch

Explain how the pericardium's normal friction-reducing fluid can, in excess, become life-threatening.

A. The serous fluid normally lets the heart glide, but too much fluid causes cardiac tamponade, compressing the heart and limiting its filling.

core Heart chambers and septa cv-chambers5r · 2q · 0s

cv-chambers-r1DOK 1recall

Name the four chambers of the heart.

A. The right and left atria and the right and left ventricles.

cv-chambers-r2DOK 1recall

Which chamber has the thickest wall, and why?

A. The left ventricle, because it pumps blood to the whole body through the aorta.

cv-chambers-r3DOK 1recall

What is the fossa ovalis, and what is it a remnant of?

A. The oval depression in the interatrial septum, the remnant of the fetal foramen ovale.

cv-chambers-r4DOK 2recall

Compare the walls of the atria and the ventricles, and why they differ.

A. The atria are thin-walled receiving chambers; the ventricles are thick-walled pumping chambers.

cv-chambers-r5DOK 3recall

Why must the interventricular septum be intact for the two circuits to stay separate?

A. It keeps oxygen-poor right-side blood from mixing with oxygen-rich left-side blood.

cv-chambers-q1DOK 1mcq

Which heart chamber has the thickest wall?

A. Right atrium

B. Left atrium

C. Right ventricle

D. Left ventricle

The left ventricle is thickest because it pumps to the whole body. The atria are thin receiving chambers and the right ventricle pumps only to the lungs.

cv-chambers-q2DOK 2mcq

The fossa ovalis is a remnant of which fetal structure?

A. Ductus arteriosus

B. Foramen ovale

C. Ductus venosus

D. Umbilical vein

The fossa ovalis marks the closed fetal foramen ovale in the interatrial septum. The other structures leave different adult remnants.

core Heart valves cv-valves5r · 2q · 0s

cv-valves-r1DOK 1recall

Name the two atrioventricular valves and where each is.

A. The tricuspid valve (right atrium to right ventricle) and the mitral (bicuspid) valve (left atrium to left ventricle).

cv-valves-r2DOK 1recall

Name the two semilunar valves and where each is.

A. The pulmonary valve (right ventricle to pulmonary trunk) and the aortic valve (left ventricle to aorta).

cv-valves-r3DOK 1recall

How many cusps does the tricuspid valve have, and the mitral valve?

A. The tricuspid has three cusps; the mitral has two.

cv-valves-r4DOK 2recall

What do the AV valves prevent, and what do the semilunar valves prevent?

A. The AV valves prevent backflow into the atria; the semilunar valves prevent backflow into the ventricles.

cv-valves-r5DOK 3recall

A valve fails to close cleanly and blood slips backward, heard as a murmur. Which structural failure at an AV valve can cause this?

A. Damage to a papillary muscle or the chordae tendineae, which normally hold the valve shut.

cv-valves-q1DOK 1mcq

Which valve lies between the left atrium and left ventricle?

A. Tricuspid valve

B. Mitral valve

C. Pulmonary valve

D. Aortic valve

The mitral (bicuspid) valve is the left AV valve. The tricuspid is the right AV valve, and the semilunar valves guard the arteries.

cv-valves-q2DOK 2mcq

The semilunar valves prevent backflow into which chambers?

A. The atria

B. The ventricles

C. The venae cavae

D. The lungs

The semilunar (pulmonary and aortic) valves stop blood returning into the ventricles. The AV valves prevent backflow into the atria.

core Pathway of blood through the heart cv-blood-pathway5r · 2q · 1s

cv-blood-pathway-r1DOK 1recall

From where does the right atrium receive blood?

A. From the body through the superior and inferior venae cavae, and from the heart wall through the coronary sinus.

cv-blood-pathway-r2DOK 1recall

Which circuit does the right side of the heart drive, and which does the left drive?

A. The right side drives the pulmonary circuit to the lungs; the left side drives the systemic circuit to the body.

cv-blood-pathway-r3DOK 1recall

What returns oxygen-rich blood to the left atrium?

A. The four pulmonary veins.

cv-blood-pathway-r4DOK 2recall

Going from the right atrium to the pulmonary trunk, which two valves does blood cross in order?

A. The tricuspid valve, then the pulmonary valve.

cv-blood-pathway-r5DOK 3recall

Trace one drop of blood from the right atrium out to the body, naming chambers and valves in order.

A. Right atrium, tricuspid valve, right ventricle, pulmonary valve, lungs, left atrium, mitral valve, left ventricle, aortic valve, aorta.

cv-blood-pathway-q1DOK 1mcq

What returns oxygen-rich blood to the left atrium?

A. Venae cavae

B. Pulmonary veins

C. Coronary sinus

D. Aorta

The four pulmonary veins return oxygen-rich blood from the lungs to the left atrium. The venae cavae and coronary sinus feed the right atrium.

cv-blood-pathway-q2DOK 2mcq

Leaving the right ventricle, which valve does blood cross next?

A. Tricuspid valve

B. Pulmonary valve

C. Mitral valve

D. Aortic valve

Blood leaves the right ventricle through the pulmonary valve into the pulmonary trunk. The tricuspid is upstream, and the mitral and aortic valves are on the left side.

cv-blood-pathway-s1DOK 3stretch

A patient's mitral valve leaks. Trace where blood abnormally goes during a left ventricular contraction, and name the valve and supports that failed.

A. It slips back from the left ventricle into the left atrium; the mitral valve failed, its cusps not held shut by the chordae tendineae and papillary muscles.

core Coronary circulation cv-coronary5r · 2q · 1s

cv-coronary-r1DOK 1recall

Where do the coronary arteries arise?

A. From the base of the aorta.

cv-coronary-r2DOK 1recall

Name the two coronary arteries.

A. The right coronary artery and the left coronary artery.

cv-coronary-r3DOK 1recall

What does the coronary sinus do?

A. It collects the cardiac veins and empties into the right atrium.

cv-coronary-r4DOK 2recall

Trace coronary flow from the aorta back to the right atrium.

A. Aorta, coronary arteries, capillaries of the myocardium, cardiac veins, coronary sinus, right atrium.

cv-coronary-r5DOK 3recall

A coronary artery is blocked. Why does the myocardium beyond it die, and what is this called?

A. It is starved of oxygen, so the muscle dies; this is a heart attack, a myocardial infarction.

cv-coronary-q1DOK 1mcq

Where do the coronary arteries originate?

A. The base of the aorta

B. The right atrium

C. The pulmonary trunk

D. The coronary sinus

The coronary arteries arise from the base of the aorta. The coronary sinus is the vein that drains the heart wall into the right atrium.

cv-coronary-q2DOK 2mcq

A blocked coronary artery starving the myocardium causes what?

A. A heart murmur

B. A myocardial infarction

C. Cardiac tamponade

D. Bradycardia

A blocked coronary artery causes a myocardial infarction, a heart attack. A murmur is a valve sound and tamponade is external compression.

cv-coronary-s1DOK 3stretch

The right coronary artery usually supplies the SA and AV nodes. Predict one rhythm problem if it is blocked, and why.

A. A conduction or rhythm disorder such as heart block or bradycardia, because the nodes lose their blood supply.

core Cardiac conduction system cv-conduction-anat5r · 2q · 0s

cv-conduction-anat-r1DOK 1recall

What is the pacemaker of the heart, and where is it?

A. The SA (sinoatrial) node, in the wall of the right atrium near the superior vena cava.

cv-conduction-anat-r2DOK 1recall

What does the AV node do?

A. It delays the signal briefly, then passes it on to the ventricles.

cv-conduction-anat-r3DOK 1recall

What is the only electrical bridge from the atria to the ventricles?

A. The AV bundle (bundle of His).

cv-conduction-anat-r4DOK 2recall

Order the conduction pathway from the pacemaker to the ventricle walls.

A. SA node, across the atria, AV node, AV bundle, bundle branches, Purkinje fibers.

cv-conduction-anat-r5DOK 3recall

Why is the brief pause at the AV node essential?

A. It holds the signal long enough for the atria to finish emptying into the ventricles before the ventricles contract.

cv-conduction-anat-q1DOK 1mcq

What is the natural pacemaker of the heart?

A. AV node

B. SA node

C. AV bundle

D. Purkinje fibers

The SA node fires fastest and sets the heart rate. The AV node delays the signal, and the bundle and Purkinje fibers distribute it.

cv-conduction-anat-q2DOK 2mcq

Why does the signal pause at the AV node?

A. To speed the ventricles

B. To let the atria finish emptying

C. To relax the heart

D. To start the heartbeat

The AV node delay lets the atria finish emptying into the ventricles before the ventricles contract. It does not start the beat, which the SA node does.

high Chordae tendineae and papillary muscles cv-valve-support3r · 1q · 0s

cv-valve-support-r1DOK 1recall

What are the chordae tendineae?

A. Strong fibrous cords that tie the cusps of an atrioventricular valve to the ventricle wall.

cv-valve-support-r2DOK 1recall

What are the papillary muscles?

A. Cone-shaped projections of the ventricle wall that anchor the chordae tendineae.

cv-valve-support-r3DOK 2recall

How do the chordae tendineae and papillary muscles keep an AV valve from failing?

A. They hold the valve cusps shut against the pressure of a contracting ventricle.

cv-valve-support-q1DOK 2mcq

What holds an atrioventricular valve shut against ventricular pressure?

A. The semilunar cusps

B. The chordae tendineae and papillary muscles

C. The endocardium

D. The interventricular septum

Chordae tendineae anchored by papillary muscles hold the AV valve cusps shut. The semilunar valves and septum are unrelated to this.

high Cardiac muscle and intercalated disc cv-cardiac-muscle3r · 1q · 0s

cv-cardiac-muscle-r1DOK 1recall

What are intercalated discs?

A. The specialized junctions where neighboring cardiac muscle cells meet end to end.

cv-cardiac-muscle-r2DOK 2recall

Which two junctions are in an intercalated disc, and what does each do?

A. Desmosomes anchor the cells so they do not pull apart; gap junctions let the contraction signal pass from cell to cell.

cv-cardiac-muscle-r3DOK 2recall

How is a cardiomyocyte described, and why is it packed with mitochondria?

A. A short, branching cell with usually one central nucleus, packed with mitochondria to fuel its constant, lifelong work.

cv-cardiac-muscle-q1DOK 2mcq

Which junction in an intercalated disc passes the contraction signal cell to cell?

A. Desmosome

B. Gap junction

C. Tight junction

D. Hemidesmosome

Gap junctions let the signal pass between cardiac cells. Desmosomes anchor the cells, and the other junctions are not part of the intercalated disc.

support Nerve supply to the heart cv-cardiac-nerves1r · 0q · 0s

cv-cardiac-nerves-r1DOK 1recall

How do the sympathetic and parasympathetic nerves affect the heart rate?

A. Sympathetic nerves speed the heart rate; parasympathetic fibers in the vagus nerve slow it.
