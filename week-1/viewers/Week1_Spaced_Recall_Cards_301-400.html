W1-301 Cell Junctions Definition DOK 1 Diff 2

What is a tight junction?

Show answer

A junction that fuses adjacent cell membranes to seal the gap between cells so nothing leaks between them.

cell junctions; tight junction

W1-302 Cell Junctions Identification DOK 1 Diff 2

Which proteins form tight junctions?

Show answer

Claudins and occludins.

cell junctions; tight junction

W1-303 Cell Junctions Structure-function DOK 2 Diff 3

Why does the blood-brain barrier depend on tight junctions?

Show answer

Tight junctions seal the spaces between cells, so substances cannot slip between them into the brain.

cell junctions; tight junction; blood-brain barrier

W1-304 Cell Junctions Definition DOK 1 Diff 2

What is a desmosome?

Show answer

A plaque-like junction that rivets neighboring cells together to resist pulling forces.

cell junctions; desmosome

W1-305 Cell Junctions Structure-function DOK 2 Diff 3

To which cytoskeletal element does a desmosome anchor, and why does that suit its job?

Show answer

Intermediate filaments, the tension-resisting fibers, which lets desmosomes hold cells together against pulling forces.

cell junctions; desmosome; intermediate filaments

W1-306 Cell Junctions Comparison DOK 2 Diff 4

Compare a desmosome and a hemidesmosome by what each one anchors.

Show answer

A desmosome rivets one cell to an adjacent cell; a hemidesmosome anchors a cell to the basement membrane beneath it.

cell junctions; desmosome; hemidesmosome

W1-307 Cell Junctions Definition DOK 1 Diff 2

What is a gap junction?

Show answer

Protein channels that let ions and small molecules pass directly between neighboring cells for communication.

cell junctions; gap junction

W1-308 Cell Junctions Structure-function DOK 2 Diff 3

What protein forms the channels of a gap junction, and what passes through them?

Show answer

Connexins form the channels, which pass ions and small molecules between cells.

cell junctions; gap junction; connexins

W1-309 Cell Junctions Comparison DOK 2 Diff 4

Compare tight junctions and gap junctions by function.

Show answer

Tight junctions seal the space between cells so nothing passes; gap junctions open channels so cells can communicate.

cell junctions; tight junction; gap junction

W1-310 Cell Junctions Structure-function DOK 2 Diff 3

What does an adherens junction do, and to which cytoskeletal element does it attach?

Show answer

It forms an adhesion belt that helps surfaces resist separation during contraction, attaching to microfilaments.

cell junctions; adherens junction; microfilaments

W1-311 Cell Junctions Classification DOK 3 Diff 5

W1-312 Cell Junctions Clinical anatomy DOK 3 Diff 5

W1-313 Cell Junctions Board-style DOK 3 Diff 6

glandular epithelium; endocrine

W1-321 Connective Tissue Identification DOK 1 Diff 2

Cells, fibers, and ground substance.

W1-322 Connective Tissue Definition DOK 1 Diff 2

The fibers and ground substance together, where most of the tissue volume sits.

W1-323 Connective Tissue Structure-function DOK 2 Diff 4

Why is connective tissue described as mostly extracellular matrix rather than cells?

Its cells are spread far apart, and fibers plus ground substance make up most of the tissue volume.

connective tissue; extracellular matrix

W1-324 Connective Tissue Definition DOK 1 Diff 2

What is ground substance?

Show answer

The gel that fills the space between cells and fibers and holds tissue fluid.

connective tissue; ground substance

W1-325 Connective Tissue Identification DOK 2 Diff 3

Collagen fibers are strong and ropelike, elastic fibers stretch and recoil, and reticular fibers form fine branching networks.

connective tissue; fibers

W1-327 Connective Tissue Fibers Identification DOK 1 Diff 2

W1-328 Connective Tissue Fibers Structure-function DOK 2 Diff 4

Elastic fibers stretch and then recoil to their original shape, letting these tissues spring back.

connective tissue; elastic fibers

W1-329 Connective Tissue Fibers Structure-function DOK 2 Diff 3

connective tissue; reticular fibers

W1-330 Connective Tissue Fibers Comparison DOK 2 Diff 3

Collagen fibers are thick and ropelike; reticular fibers are fine and branching.
