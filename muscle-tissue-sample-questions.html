Three depths, three colors: navy Recall (Core), gold Application (Mechanism, majors), terra Reasoning (Clinical, majors).

Core

Recall

Name it or define it.

Name the three types of muscle tissue.

Model answer: Skeletal, cardiac, and smooth.

Name the three connective-tissue sheaths from outer to inner.

Model answer: Epimysium (whole muscle), perimysium (fascicle), endomysium (fiber).

What is the contractile unit of a myofibril?

Model answer: The sarcomere, running Z disc to Z disc.

Mechanism

Application

Use it or explain how a structure fits its job.

During contraction the A band stays the same length but the I band and H zone shrink. What does that tell you?

Model answer: The filaments slide past each other rather than shortening, which is the sliding filament model.

Why does the T-tubule and triad arrangement matter for a fast contraction?

Model answer: The T-tubule carries the signal deep into the fiber and the triad couples it to the SR so calcium is released everywhere at once.

How does tropomyosin control when myosin can bind actin?

Model answer: At rest tropomyosin blocks the binding sites; when calcium binds troponin, tropomyosin moves and exposes them.

Clinical

Reasoning

Predict or analyze from structure.

In Duchenne muscular dystrophy dystrophin is defective. Reason why the fiber tears with use.

Model answer: Dystrophin links the filaments to the sarcolemma; without it the membrane is fragile and tears during contraction.

In myasthenia gravis ACh receptors are destroyed. Predict the effect and why.

Model answer: The end plate cannot receive the signal, so the muscle is weak and fatigues even though the nerve fires normally.

Why does an endurance athlete rely on Type I fibers rather than Type IIb?

Model answer: Type I fibers are red, rich in mitochondria, and fatigue-resistant, suited to sustained low-power work.
