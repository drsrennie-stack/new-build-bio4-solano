1. Functions and organization

Overview
  1. Name the four processes of respiration and state where each one happens. Recall
  2. Which two processes are the job of the respiratory system itself? Recall
  3. How does cellular respiration differ from the work of the respiratory system, and which organelle carries it out? Apply
  4. Divide the airway two ways: upper versus lower tract, and conducting versus respiratory zone. Which structures fall in each? Apply
  5. Why will the body fail if either the respiratory or the cardiovascular system stops working? Reason

2. Thoracic cavity

Location
  1. Where do the lungs sit relative to the mediastinum? Recall
  2. Name the structures the mediastinum contains between the lungs. Recall
  3. What forms the floor of the thoracic cavity, and what does it separate? Apply

3. External nose and nasal cavity

Upper tract
  1. Name the external features of the nose (root, bridge, dorsum, apex, nares, alae). Recall
  2. Which bones and cartilages form the skeletal framework of the nose? Recall
  3. What forms the nasal septum anteriorly and posteriorly, and what are the choanae? Apply
  4. Identify the roof, floor, and vestibule of the nasal cavity. Apply
  5. List the functions of the nose beyond simply moving air. Reason

4. Mucosa, conchae, and sinuses

Upper tract
  1. Contrast olfactory mucosa with respiratory mucosa (location and epithelium). Recall
  2. What do lysozyme and defensins do, and how do cilia move the mucus sheet? Apply
  3. Name the three conchae and the meatuses, and explain how they condition inhaled and exhaled air. Apply
  4. Which bones contain the paranasal sinuses, and what are their functions? Recall
  5. Distinguish rhinitis from sinusitis. Apply
  6. Why is the nasal mucosa prone to nosebleeds and to a runny nose in cold weather? Reason

5. The pharynx

Upper tract
  1. Name the three regions of the pharynx from superior to inferior. Recall
  2. For each region, state whether it carries air only or both air and food, and name its tonsils. Apply
  3. Why does the epithelium change from pseudostratified columnar to stratified squamous as you move down the pharynx? Reason

6. The larynx

Larynx
  1. List the nine laryngeal cartilages and state which one is elastic rather than hyaline. Recall
  2. What does the cricoid attach to, and which cartilages anchor the vocal folds? Recall
  3. Distinguish the true vocal folds from the vestibular (false) folds, and define the glottis. Apply
  4. Why does the epithelium differ above versus below the vocal cords? Apply
  5. Explain how the epiglottis and the vocal folds protect the lower airway during a swallow. Reason
  6. How do the vocal folds act as a sphincter in the Valsalva maneuver? Reason

7. Trachea and histology

Lower tract
  1. Name the four layers of the trachea from inside to outside. Recall
  2. Describe the C-shaped rings: what tissue, how many, which way the opening faces, and what bridges it. Apply
  3. What is the carina, and why is it so sensitive? Apply
  4. Identify the respiratory epithelium and explain how goblet cells and cilia work together. Apply
  5. How does the trachealis muscle help during a cough? Reason
  6. Name the airway and alveolar cell types and the job of each: ciliated, goblet, basal, type I, type II, and alveolar macrophage. Apply

8. The bronchial tree

Lower tract
  1. Trace the branching from the trachea to the bronchioles, naming each order of bronchus. Recall
  2. How does the right main bronchus differ from the left, and why does it matter clinically? Apply
  3. Describe the three structural changes (cartilage, epithelium, smooth muscle) as the airways get smaller. Apply

9. Respiratory zone and the alveolus

Gas exchange
  1. Name the structures of the respiratory zone in order, and state where gas exchange actually occurs. Recall
  2. What two walls make up the respiratory membrane, and which cells form the alveolar wall? Apply
  3. What do type II alveolar cells secrete, and why is it essential? Apply
  4. What are alveolar macrophages and alveolar pores for? Recall
  5. Predict what happens to the lungs of a premature infant who lacks surfactant. Reason
  6. Name the three layers of the respiratory membrane. Recall
  7. What is an alveolar sac? Recall

10. The lungs and pleurae

Lungs
  1. Give the lobe and fissure counts for the right and left lungs, and explain the cardiac notch. Recall
  2. Identify the apex, base, costal surface, and hilum, and name what enters at the hilum. Apply
  3. Distinguish the parietal pleura, visceral pleura, and pleural cavity. Apply
  4. What is a bronchopulmonary segment, and why does its independence matter surgically? Reason
  5. Why does losing the seal of the pleural cavity (pneumothorax) collapse the lung? Reason
  6. What is the lingula, and which lobe carries it? Recall
  7. What is the costodiaphragmatic recess, and why does it matter for a thoracentesis? Apply

11. Blood supply, innervation, and diaphragm

Support
  1. Contrast the pulmonary and bronchial circulations by pressure, volume, and purpose. Apply
  2. Which nerves innervate the lungs? Recall
  3. Name the diaphragm's nerve, the cavities it separates, and the muscles that assist forced breathing. Apply
  4. Name the three openings of the diaphragm with their vertebral levels. Recall
