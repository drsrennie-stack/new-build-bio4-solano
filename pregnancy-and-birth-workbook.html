Placenta and cord 3 questions

01 DOK 1 · Recall

What is the placenta, and what two tissues form it?

Reveal answer

The placenta is the temporary organ of pregnancy that exchanges materials between mother and fetus. It is built from fetal tissue (chorionic villi, derived from the trophoblast) and maternal tissue (the endometrium, which becomes the decidua basalis during pregnancy). Maternal and fetal blood do not mix; they exchange across a thin membrane.

02 DOK 1 · Recall

List the major functions of the placenta.

Reveal answer

Gas exchange (O2 from mother to fetus, CO2 the other way), nutrient and waste exchange, hormone production (hCG, progesterone, estrogens, hPL), and barrier protection (against many but not all infections and substances).

03 DOK 1 · Recall

Describe the umbilical cord.

Reveal answer

The umbilical cord connects the fetus to the placenta. It contains two umbilical arteries (carrying deoxygenated blood from fetus to placenta) and one umbilical vein (carrying oxygenated blood from placenta to fetus). The vessels are embedded in Wharton's jelly, a connective tissue that protects them from compression.

Fetal circulation adaptations 2 questions

04 DOK 2 · Apply

Name the three fetal circulatory shunts and what each bypasses.

Reveal answer

Ductus venosus: bypasses the liver, sending oxygenated blood from the umbilical vein directly into the inferior vena cava. Foramen ovale: shunts blood from right atrium to left atrium, bypassing the non-functioning lungs. Ductus arteriosus: shunts blood from the pulmonary trunk to the aorta, bypassing the lungs.

05 DOK 2 · Apply

What happens to the fetal shunts at birth, and why?

Reveal answer

With the first breath, lungs inflate and pulmonary resistance drops; left atrial pressure rises. The foramen ovale closes functionally (becomes the fossa ovalis). The ductus arteriosus constricts and becomes the ligamentum arteriosum. The ductus venosus closes, becoming the ligamentum venosum. The umbilical vein closes, becoming the ligamentum teres of the liver. The shunts were necessary when the placenta was the gas exchange organ; they become unnecessary as soon as the lungs take over.

Labor and birth 2 questions

06 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the three stages of labor.

Reveal answer

Stage 1: dilation. Cervix thins (effaces) and dilates to 10 cm. Stage 2: expulsion. Strong uterine contractions push the fetus through the birth canal. Stage 3: placental. After the baby is born, the placenta detaches and is delivered.

07 DOK 2 · Apply

What features of the female pelvis make labor possible?

Reveal answer

Wider pelvic inlet and outlet, broader pubic arch (about 80-90 degrees vs about 50-60 in males), more curved sacrum that lifts away from the canal, lighter overall structure. All these shape the bony passage so a fetal head can negotiate it.
