Overview 2 questions

01 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the two divisions of the ANS and describe what each generally does.

Reveal answer

Sympathetic: 'fight or flight' division; mobilizes the body during stress or activity (increases heart rate, dilates pupils, redirects blood from gut to muscles, dilates airways). Parasympathetic: 'rest and digest' division; conserves energy and runs the body at rest (slows heart, constricts pupils, stimulates digestion). The two oppose each other in most organs.

02 DOK 1 · Recall

How is the autonomic motor pathway different from the somatic motor pathway?

Reveal answer

Somatic: one motor neuron runs from the CNS directly to the skeletal muscle. Autonomic: two neurons in series. The first (preganglionic) neuron's cell body is in the CNS; its axon synapses in a peripheral ganglion. The second (postganglionic) neuron's cell body is in the ganglion; its axon innervates the target organ.

Sympathetic division 3 questions

03 DOK 1 · Recall

Where does the sympathetic division originate, and what is its alternative name?

Reveal answer

From the thoracic and upper lumbar segments of the spinal cord (T1-L2). It is also called the thoracolumbar division for that reason.

04 DOK 1 · Recall

Where are sympathetic ganglia located, and what is the sympathetic chain?

Reveal answer

Sympathetic ganglia sit close to the spinal cord, in the sympathetic trunks (chain ganglia) running alongside the vertebral column, and in additional prevertebral ganglia anterior to the aorta (celiac, superior mesenteric, inferior mesenteric). The two sympathetic chains (one on each side of the vertebral column) extend from the cervical to the coccygeal level, even though sympathetic outflow comes from only T1-L2.

05 DOK 2 · Apply

Compare sympathetic preganglionic and postganglionic axons by length and explain why.

Reveal answer

Sympathetic preganglionic axons are short, because the ganglia (chain or prevertebral) sit close to the spinal cord. Sympathetic postganglionic axons are long, because they then travel from the ganglion to distant target organs. The pattern is the opposite of the parasympathetic system.

Parasympathetic division 4 questions

06 DOK 1 · Recall

Where does the parasympathetic division originate, and what is its alternative name?

Reveal answer

From the brainstem (via certain cranial nerves) and the sacral spinal cord (S2-S4). It is also called the craniosacral division.

07 DOK 1 · Recall

Where are parasympathetic ganglia located?

Reveal answer

Right next to or within the wall of the target organ. They are called terminal or intramural ganglia for this reason.

08 DOK 2 · Apply

Compare parasympathetic preganglionic and postganglionic axons by length, and explain why.

Reveal answer

Parasympathetic preganglionic axons are long, because the ganglia are far from the CNS (in the target organ). Parasympathetic postganglionic axons are short, because they only travel from the wall of the organ to its tissue. Opposite of the sympathetic pattern.

09 DOK 1 · Recall

Which cranial nerve carries most of the parasympathetic outflow to the thorax and abdomen?

Reveal answer

The vagus nerve (CN X). It supplies parasympathetic fibers to the heart, lungs, and most of the abdominal viscera down to about the splenic flexure of the colon. Below that level, sacral parasympathetic fibers (S2-S4) take over via the pelvic splanchnic nerves.
