Ovaries 3 questions

01 DOK 1 · Recall

Where do the ovaries sit, and what holds them in place?

Reveal answer

In the lateral pelvis, against the lateral pelvic wall. They are held by the ovarian ligament (to the uterus), the suspensory ligament (to the pelvic wall, carrying the ovarian artery and vein), and the broad ligament (a peritoneal fold that drapes over the uterine tubes and ovaries).

02 DOK 1 · Recall

What are the ovaries' two main functions?

Reveal answer

Producing oocytes (gametogenesis) and secreting female sex hormones (estrogens and progesterone). Both happen in the cortex, where follicles develop.

03 DOK 1 · Recall

What is a follicle, and what are its developmental stages?

Reveal answer

A follicle is an oocyte and its surrounding support cells inside the ovary. Stages: primordial follicle (one layer of squamous follicular cells), primary follicle (cuboidal cells, becomes multilayered), secondary follicle (developing fluid-filled antrum), mature (Graafian) follicle (large antrum, ready to ovulate). After ovulation, the ruptured follicle becomes the corpus luteum, which makes progesterone.

Uterine tubes 3 questions

04 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the regions of the uterine (fallopian) tube from ovary to uterus.

Reveal answer

Fimbriae (finger-like projections that drape over the ovary), infundibulum (funnel-shaped region holding the fimbriae), ampulla (the wide middle portion where fertilization usually occurs), isthmus (narrow region near the uterus), uterine part (passes through the uterine wall).

05 DOK 2 · Apply

What lines the uterine tubes, and how does the lining help an ovulated ovum reach the uterus?

Reveal answer

Ciliated simple columnar epithelium with scattered non-ciliated secretory cells. The cilia beat in waves toward the uterus, drawing the ovum (which has no power of its own) into and through the tube. Smooth muscle in the wall also contributes peristaltic contractions.

06 DOK 3 · Reason

An ectopic pregnancy is implantation outside the uterus, most often in the ampulla of a uterine tube. Predict why this is dangerous and why it cannot continue as a normal pregnancy.

Reveal answer

The tube is small in diameter and not built to expand around a growing embryo. As the embryo grows, the tube stretches and eventually ruptures, producing severe internal bleeding into the peritoneal cavity. The embryo also cannot survive in this location for similar reasons. Surgery or medical termination is urgent.

Uterus 4 questions

07 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the regions of the uterus.

Reveal answer

Fundus (the dome above the entrance of the uterine tubes), body (the main midsection), isthmus (narrow region between body and cervix), and cervix (the narrow neck that opens into the vagina). The cavity inside is the uterine cavity, opening to the vagina through the cervical canal.

08 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the three layers of the uterine wall.

Reveal answer

Perimetrium (outer serosa, peritoneum), myometrium (thick middle smooth muscle layer that contracts during labor), and endometrium (inner mucosa with simple columnar epithelium and abundant uterine glands).

09 DOK 2 · Apply

Compare the two functional layers of the endometrium.

Reveal answer

Stratum functionalis: the upper layer that thickens, develops glands and vessels, and is shed each month during menstruation if no pregnancy occurs. Stratum basalis: the deeper layer that remains and regenerates the functionalis each cycle. The cycle depends on this two-layer arrangement.

10 DOK 1 · Recall

What is the normal position of the uterus relative to the bladder and rectum?

Reveal answer

The uterus is typically anteverted (tilted forward) over the bladder. Posteriorly it relates to the rectum, with the rectouterine pouch (cul-de-sac of Douglas) between them — the lowest point of the female peritoneal cavity, where fluid or blood tends to collect.

Vagina and external genitalia 2 questions

11 DOK 1 · Recall

Describe the vagina.

Reveal answer

A muscular tube about 8-10 cm long extending from the cervix to the vaginal opening. Lined by non-keratinized stratified squamous epithelium that resists friction. Walls are highly distensible. Functions: receives the penis during intercourse, serves as the birth canal, and provides the exit route for menstrual flow.

12 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the external female genital structures collectively known as the vulva.

Reveal answer

Mons pubis (fatty pad over the pubic symphysis), labia majora (paired outer folds), labia minora (paired inner folds), clitoris (erectile organ at the anterior junction of the labia minora), vestibule (the cleft between the labia minora containing the urethral and vaginal openings), and the greater vestibular (Bartholin's) glands.

Breast 1 question

13 DOK 1 · Recall

Describe the structure of the female breast.

Reveal answer

Each breast contains 15-20 lobes radiating around the nipple, each subdivided into lobules. Lobules contain alveoli (clusters of milk-secreting cells active during lactation). Each lobe drains via a lactiferous duct that widens to a lactiferous sinus near the nipple and then opens at the nipple. Connective tissue suspensory (Cooper's) ligaments anchor the breast to the skin and underlying chest wall.
