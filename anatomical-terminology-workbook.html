Position, planes, and directional terms 4 questions

01 DOK 1 · Recall

Define anatomical position.

Reveal answer

Standing upright, eyes forward, feet flat and slightly apart, arms at the sides with palms facing forward (thumbs pointing laterally). Every anatomical directional term assumes this position regardless of how the body is currently oriented.

02 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the three primary anatomical planes and how each cuts the body.

Reveal answer

Sagittal: vertically into right and left portions (midsagittal at the midline, parasagittal off the midline). Frontal (coronal): vertically into anterior and posterior portions. Transverse (horizontal): horizontally into superior and inferior portions. An oblique plane is any angle other than these three.

03 DOK 1 · Recall

Define these directional pairs and give an example use for each: anterior/posterior, medial/lateral, superior/inferior, superficial/deep.

Reveal answer

Anterior/posterior (ventral/dorsal): front/back; the heart is anterior to the spine. Medial/lateral: toward/away from midline; the sternum is medial to the nipple. Superior/inferior: toward head/toward feet; the heart is superior to the diaphragm. Superficial/deep: closer to/farther from surface; the skin is superficial to muscle.

04 DOK 2 · Apply

Define proximal and distal, and explain why they differ from superior and inferior.

Reveal answer

Proximal: closer to the trunk or to the point of attachment of a limb. Distal: farther. They are limb-relative, not body-relative, so they stay correct when the limb moves. The wrist is distal to the elbow whether the arm hangs down or is raised overhead; the same is not true of superior and inferior.

Regional terms 4 questions

05 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the regional terms for the major parts of the head.

Reveal answer

Cephalic (head as a whole), frontal (forehead), orbital (eye socket area), nasal (nose), oral (mouth), buccal (cheek), mental (chin), otic (ear), occipital (back of head).

06 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the regional terms for the major regions of the trunk.

Reveal answer

Cervical (neck), thoracic (chest), sternal (sternum), mammary (breast), abdominal (abdomen), umbilical (navel), pelvic (pelvis), inguinal (groin), vertebral (spine), lumbar (lower back), sacral (between hips), gluteal (buttocks).

07 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the regional terms for the upper limb.

Reveal answer

Acromial (shoulder point), axillary (armpit), brachial (arm), antecubital (front of elbow), olecranal (back of elbow), antebrachial (forearm), carpal (wrist), manus (hand), palmar (palm), pollex (thumb), digital (fingers).

08 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the regional terms for the lower limb.

Reveal answer

Coxal (hip), femoral (thigh), patellar (kneecap), popliteal (back of knee), crural (leg, front), sural (calf), fibular (lateral leg), tarsal (ankle), calcaneal (heel), pedal (foot), plantar (sole), hallux (great toe), digital (toes).
