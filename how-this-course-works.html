Get the picture right

One idea trips most students up. Fix it now.

✓ This is how it works

Prep tonight, for tomorrow

Tonight you complete the materials for the next class session. You walk in already knowing the terms, having watched the videos, and having drawn the structures. Class is where you put that to work with your team.

✗ This is the trap

Wait, then catch up after

Treating the materials as homework due after class means you arrive cold. The activities are built on prep you have not done yet, so the day is confusing and you fall a step behind. In an eight-week intensive, that gap grows fast.

Your evening routine

Each night, prepare for the next day.

Every class night follows the same five steps. Tick them off as you go. When a card turns and says you are ready, you have done everything you need for tomorrow. These boxes are just for you; nothing is recorded.

Questions, answered

What students usually ask first.

So the prework is due the night before, not after class?+

Yes. If the schedule says tomorrow is Tuesday, you do Tuesday's materials tonight, so you can apply them in tomorrow's lab and class. Nothing in this course is meant to be done after the class it belongs to.

How much time should preparing take?+

Plan for focused time each class night: the videos, a notes review, your RecallRx cards, and the required drawings. The exact load is set in the packet for the week. Doing it nightly keeps each session short and keeps you from falling behind.

What if I fall behind one night?+

Start with tomorrow's materials before the next class, then backfill. Staying ready for the class in front of you matters more than catching up perfectly on what already passed. In an eight-week intensive, the next session always assumes the prep is done.

Where do I find what is assigned for each day?+

Follow the instructions in your packet for this week, and open the current week from the course home page. Each day lists its videos, notes, cards, and drawings.

Stay on track

Prepared tonight means ready tomorrow.

Open your current week, find tomorrow's materials, and work through the five steps. Do that each night and the whole course stays manageable.
