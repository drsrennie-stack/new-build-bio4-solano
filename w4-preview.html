Name the three connective tissue coverings of a skeletal muscle, from the whole muscle in to the single fiber.

A. Epimysium (whole muscle), perimysium (each fascicle), endomysium (each fiber).

w4-ct-coverings-r2DOK 1recall

What does the epimysium wrap, and what tissue is it?

A. The entire muscle; dense irregular connective tissue.

w4-ct-coverings-r3DOK 1recall

What does the endomysium wrap, and what tissue is it?

A. Each individual muscle fiber; a fine areolar connective tissue with reticular fibers.

w4-ct-coverings-r4DOK 2recall

How do the three muscle sheaths connect a fiber's pull to the bone?

A. They are continuous with one another and merge into the tendon, so the pull reaches the bone.

w4-ct-coverings-r5DOK 3recall

A muscle strain tears fibers within the belly while a tendon rupture separates muscle from bone. Which connective tissue continuity explains why one pull can injure either site?

A. The endomysium, perimysium, and epimysium are continuous with the tendon, so force runs from fiber to bone along one connected sheath.

w4-ct-coverings-q1DOK 1mcq

Which connective tissue sheath wraps the entire muscle?

A. Endomysium

B. Perimysium

C. Epimysium

D. Tendon

Epimysium wraps the whole muscle; perimysium wraps each fascicle and endomysium each fiber. The tendon is the merged continuation that attaches to bone.

w4-muscle-organization-r5DOK 3recall

A. The muscle fiber, since the muscle and fascicle are groups of cells.

w4-muscle-organization-q1DOK 1mcq

A. Muscle, fascicle, fiber, myofibril, myofilament

B. Fiber, muscle, fascicle, myofibril, myofilament

C. Muscle, fiber, fascicle, myofilament, myofibril

D. Fascicle, muscle, fiber, myofibril, myofilament

The nesting is muscle, fascicle, fiber, myofibril, myofilament. A fiber is a single cell; a fascicle is a bundle of fibers within the whole muscle.

core Lymph and its pathway w4-lymph-pathway5r · 1q · 0s

w4-lymph-pathway-r1DOK 1recall

What is lymph?

A. Interstitial fluid once it has entered the lymphatic vessels; a clear, watery fluid.

w4-lymph-pathway-r2DOK 1recall

Trace the pathway of lymph from the tissues back to the blood.

A. Lymphatic capillaries, collecting vessels, lymph nodes, lymphatic trunks, lymphatic ducts, then the subclavian veins.

w4-lymph-pathway-r3DOK 1recall

Which two ducts return lymph to the blood, and where do they empty?

A. The right lymphatic duct and the thoracic duct, at the junction of the subclavian and internal jugular veins.

w4-lymph-pathway-r4DOK 2recall

Compare the drainage areas of the right lymphatic duct and the thoracic duct.

A. The right lymphatic duct drains the upper right quadrant; the thoracic duct drains the rest of the body.

w4-lymph-pathway-r5DOK 3recall

The lymphatic system has no pump, yet lymph still moves toward the heart. How, and what keeps it one-way?

A. Nearby skeletal muscle squeeze and breathing push it along, and valves keep it flowing in one direction.

w4-lymph-pathway-q1DOK 2mcq

Where does lymph re-enter the bloodstream?

A. At the spleen

B. At the subclavian veins

C. At the aorta

D. At the thymus

Lymph returns where the ducts empty at the subclavian and internal jugular vein junction. The spleen and thymus are lymphatic organs, not the return point.
