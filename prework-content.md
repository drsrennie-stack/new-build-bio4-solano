# BIO 004 Pre-Work Content

Material pulled from the concept pages, to be built into the pre-work. The
drawing-based synthesis exercises ("Given, not Googled") live here, not on the
reference pages. Append a section here each time a concept page is built.

---

## Body Cavities and Regions

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw the thorax in cross-section at the level of the sternal angle. Mark the line
that splits the superior from the inferior mediastinum, then box the anterior,
middle, and posterior compartments and write one structure into each. On a second
sketch, draw the posterior abdominal wall and place the SADPUCKER organs against
it, then add three intraperitoneal organs floating on their mesenteries.

---

## The Language of Anatomy

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw a figure in anatomical position. Label it with at least twelve regional
terms, six on the anterior surface and six on the posterior. Then draw the three
main planes through the figure and name the view each one produces.

---

## Histology: The Four Tissue Types

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw the four tissue types as four boxes. In each box write its function, one
location, and one defining structural feature. Then draw arrows from the three
germ layers, ectoderm, mesoderm, and endoderm, to the tissues each one forms.

---

## Anatomy of the Cell

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw a generalized cell. Label the plasma membrane, the nucleus, and at least
eight organelles, and write the one main job of each beside it. Then circle every
organelle bounded by a membrane.

---

## Integumentary and Skin

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw a block of skin in cross-section. Label the epidermis, dermis, and
hypodermis, then the five epidermal strata in order. Add one hair follicle with
its arrector pili and one of each gland, then mark how deep a first, second, and
third degree burn would reach.

---

## Bone Histology

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw a long bone in longitudinal section. Label the diaphysis, epiphyses,
metaphysis, medullary cavity, periosteum, endosteum, articular cartilage, and the
epiphyseal line. Then draw one osteon in cross-section with its central canal,
lamellae, lacunae, and canaliculi.

---

## The Skull

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw the skull from the side. Label all eight cranial bones and as many facial
bones as you can see, then trace the four sutures. On a second sketch, draw the
cranial base from above and mark the foramen magnum, the sella turcica, the
cribriform plate, and three other foramina with what passes through each.

---

## The Vertebral Column and Thoracic Cage

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw a typical vertebra from above. Label the body, the vertebral arch with its
pedicles and laminae, the vertebral foramen, and the spinous, transverse, and
articular processes. Then draw the vertebral column from the side, label the four
curvatures and the five regions, and mark which curvatures are primary.

---

## The Upper Extremity

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw the bones of the upper limb in order from the shoulder to the fingertips.
Label the clavicle, scapula, humerus, radius, ulna, and the three groups of hand
bones. Then redraw the scapula and the humerus larger and label at least five
markings on each.

---

## The Lower Extremity

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw the bones of the lower limb in order from the hip to the toes. Label the hip
bone, femur, patella, tibia, fibula, and the three groups of foot bones. Then
redraw the hip bone larger, mark the three bones that fuse to form it, and label
at least five markings.

---

## Articulations and Joints

### Sketch and synthesis exercise

Draw a synovial joint in cross-section. Label the articular cartilage, joint
cavity, articular capsule, synovial membrane, and a reinforcing ligament. Then
list the six synovial joint types and name one joint in the body for each.
