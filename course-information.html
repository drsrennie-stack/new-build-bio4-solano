Academic policies & integrity

How we work together.

The short version: do your own work, ask for help when you need it, and treat AI like a study partner you do not let take your tests.

Academic integrity

  • Cheating, plagiarism, and academic dishonesty will result in at least a zero on the work in question, and may result in an F for the course. All suspected violations are reported to the V.P. of Student Services.
  • Complete your own work and cite your sources in MLA or APA format. If you need help with citations, ask.

AI policy

  • You may use generative AI only on assignments specifically designated as AI-friendly (their titles will include "AI" or the instructions will say so).
  • You may also use AI freely as a tutor, study coach, or for creating your own study sheets. I encourage that.
  • You may not use AI on exams or on graded sections of Team-Based Learning activities. A plagiarism or AI rating above 25% on a graded assignment earns an automatic zero.
  • See the AI Honor Contract for full details.

Late work and make-ups

  • All quizzes, discussion boards, exams, lab reports, and assignments are due on the scheduled date. No late work, no make-ups.
  • No make-up exams under any circumstances. Lab practicals take significant setup; if you miss one, it is a zero.
  • Missed in-class work, including TBL activities, bonus opportunities, and pop quizzes, cannot be made up. Plan to be in class.
  • Your cumulative lecture and lab practical exams can replace your lowest earlier scores (exams 1 through 4), but only if you have attempted every prior exam. Exam 5 cannot be replaced.

Lab safety & procedures

Professional conduct in the anatomy lab.

Our anatomy lab uses donor material in partnership with the UCSF Body Donation Program. The policies below protect the dignity and privacy of donors, honor our legal agreement with UCSF, and keep the lab a focused, professional learning space.

Required for every lab

Personal protective equipment

A lab coat in any color, kept in the lab until the end of the course, and non-latex healthcare gloves. No exceptions, both are required from day one.

Digital device policy in the lab

Strict, and non-negotiable. Aligned with our agreement with UCSF and the ethical standards of the UCSF Body Donation Program.

  • No phones, smartwatches, laptops, tablets, or recording devices in the dry or wet lab at any time.
  • All devices must be silenced and stored completely out of sight, not in pockets, laps, or on the table.
  • Digital note-taking, photography, video, and audio recording are strictly prohibited in lab settings.
  • Access to digital materials happens outside lab time, during open study hours or at home.
  • Accommodated device use must be pre-approved by me and by the UCSF Body Donation Program.
  • Classroom SmartPads provided for lab activities are approved and remain on-site.

Consequences

If the device policy is broken

1st offense: loss of lab quiz points for the day, no make-up; verbal warning. 2nd offense: dismissal from class for two sessions, missed work cannot be made up, and your phone is stored in a locked cabinet for the rest of the course. Repeated violations: formal referral to student conduct.

A few more lab rules

  • Doors lock once a lab exam starts. If you are late, you cannot enter.
  • You must be present for 90% of lab and lecture to count for attendance. Arriving 20 minutes late or leaving 20 minutes early counts as an absence.
  • Additional cadaver lab safety briefings will be delivered in person during the first week of lab, including handling, disposal, and emergency procedures.

By remaining enrolled in this course, you are agreeing to uphold these policies as a matter of ethical and academic integrity.
