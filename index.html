Block 1 · 5 lectures

Introduction to the Body

  • Anatomical Terminology Anatomical position, directional terms, planes, and regional terms.
  • Body Cavities and Membranes The dorsal and ventral cavities, their subdivisions, and serous membranes.
  • Cell Anatomy The plasma membrane, nucleus, and the cytoplasmic organelles.
  • Histology: The Four Tissue Types Epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous tissue.
  • The Integumentary System The epidermis, dermis, hypodermis, and the skin's accessory structures.

Block 2 · 9 lectures

Support and Control

  • Bone Histology Compact and spongy bone, the osteon, and bone cells.
  • The Axial Skeleton: The Skull The cranial and facial bones, sutures, and skull foramina.
  • The Axial Skeleton: The Vertebral Column The vertebrae, the regions of the spine, and the thoracic cage.
  • The Appendicular Skeleton: Upper Limb The pectoral girdle and the bones of the upper limb.
  • The Appendicular Skeleton: Lower Limb The pelvic girdle and the bones of the lower limb.
  • Articulations Fibrous, cartilaginous, and synovial joints and their movements.
  • Muscle Structure The skeletal muscle fiber, the sarcomere, and the contractile proteins.
  • Muscle Organization and Fascicles Connective tissue wrappings, fascicle arrangements, attachments, and levers.
  • The Endocrine System The endocrine glands and their locations.

Block 3 · 7 lectures

Transport and Exchange

  • The Heart The heart wall, chambers, valves, and the great vessels.
  • Cardiac Conduction The conduction system structures and the nerve supply of the heart.
  • Blood Plasma and the formed elements of blood.
  • Blood Vessels The three tunics and the structure of arteries, capillaries, and veins.
  • Blood Vessel Disorders and Fetal Circulation Vascular disorders and the vessels and shunts of fetal circulation.
  • The Respiratory System The airways, the larynx, the lungs, and the alveoli.
  • The Lymphatic System Lymphatic vessels, lymph nodes, and the lymphatic organs.

Block 4 · 6 lectures

Digestive, Urinary, and Reproductive Systems

  • The Alimentary Canal The wall of the digestive tube and the organs from mouth to anus.
  • The Accessory Digestive Organs The teeth, tongue, salivary glands, liver, gallbladder, and pancreas.
  • The Urinary System The kidney, the nephron, and the ureters, bladder, and urethra.
  • The Male Reproductive System The testes, the duct system, the accessory glands, and the penis.
  • The Female Reproductive System The ovaries, uterine tubes, uterus, vagina, and mammary glands.
  • Pregnancy and Birth Fertilization, implantation, the placenta, and labor.

Block 5 · 10 lectures

The Nervous System

  • Functional Organization and Nervous Tissue The divisions of the nervous system, the neuron, glia, and myelination.
  • Gross Anatomy and Neuronal Integration Gray and white matter, the collections of nervous tissue, and the reflex arc.
  • The Brain The cerebrum, diencephalon, cerebellum, limbic system, and cerebral arteries.
  • The Brainstem The midbrain, pons, medulla, and the reticular formation.
  • The Spinal Cord The external and internal anatomy of the spinal cord and its roots.
  • The Meninges and Cerebrospinal Fluid The cranial and spinal meninges, the ventricles, and CSF.
  • The Cranial Nerves The twelve cranial nerves, their classification, function, and foramina.
  • The Nerve Plexuses The cervical, brachial, lumbar, and sacral plexuses.
  • The Peripheral Nervous System Nerve structure, spinal nerves, rami, and sensory receptors.
  • The Autonomic Nervous System The two-neuron pathway, the autonomic ganglia, and the two divisions.
