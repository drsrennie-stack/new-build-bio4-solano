Salivary glands 3 questions

01 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the three pairs of major salivary glands and where each opens.

Reveal answer

Parotid glands: the largest, in front of and below each ear; ducts open into the cheek opposite the upper second molar. Submandibular glands: along the inferior border of the mandible; ducts open under the tongue near the lingual frenulum. Sublingual glands: under the tongue; multiple small ducts open into the floor of the mouth.

02 DOK 1 · Recall

What is in saliva, and what does each major component do?

Reveal answer

Water (lubricates), mucus (lubricates and coats the bolus), salivary amylase (begins starch digestion), lysozyme (antibacterial), antibodies (IgA, immune defense), bicarbonate (buffers pH), and ions (maintain oral environment).

03 DOK 3 · Reason

Mumps is a viral infection of the parotid glands. Predict why patients with mumps have pain when chewing.

Reveal answer

The parotid sits over the masseter muscle and stretches as the jaw moves. Inflammation swells the gland against its tight fibrous capsule. Chewing forces the swollen gland to move and stretch further, which is painful.

Liver 4 questions

04 DOK 1 · Recall

Where does the liver sit, and what are its functions in basic terms?

Reveal answer

The liver fills most of the right upper quadrant and extends across the midline. It produces bile (which emulsifies fats), processes nutrients absorbed from the GI tract, stores glycogen and vitamins, detoxifies drugs and metabolic waste, synthesizes plasma proteins (albumin, clotting factors), and metabolizes hormones.

05 DOK 1 · Recall

Name the lobes of the liver.

Reveal answer

The liver has four anatomical lobes. The right lobe is the largest. The left lobe is smaller and crosses the midline. The caudate lobe sits posteriorly near the inferior vena cava. The quadrate lobe sits anteriorly near the gallbladder. Functionally, the liver is also divided into right and left functional halves based on blood supply and bile drainage, which does not match the surface anatomy exactly.

06 DOK 2 · Compare

The liver has two sources of blood supply. Compare them.

Reveal answer

Hepatic artery (about 25 percent of liver blood flow): a branch of the celiac trunk; delivers oxygenated blood. Hepatic portal vein (about 75 percent of blood flow): delivers nutrient-rich, partly deoxygenated venous blood from the GI tract, spleen, and pancreas for first-pass processing. Both empty into hepatic sinusoids; processed blood leaves the liver through the hepatic veins into the inferior vena cava.

07 DOK 1 · Recall

What is a hepatic lobule, and what is the portal triad?

Reveal answer

A hepatic lobule is the functional unit of the liver, a hexagonal arrangement of hepatocytes (liver cells) radiating from a central vein. At the corners of each hexagon sits a portal triad: a small branch of the hepatic artery, a small branch of the hepatic portal vein, and a small bile duct. Blood flows from the triad inward through sinusoids; bile flows outward through canaliculi to the triad.

Gallbladder and biliary tree 3 questions

08 DOK 1 · Recall

Where is the gallbladder, what does it do, and what is the path of bile?

Reveal answer

The gallbladder sits in a depression on the inferior surface of the liver in the right upper quadrant. It stores and concentrates bile produced by the liver. Bile flows from hepatocytes through canaliculi to small bile ducts, then to the right and left hepatic ducts, which merge as the common hepatic duct. The cystic duct (from the gallbladder) joins it to form the common bile duct, which empties into the duodenum at the major duodenal papilla.

09 DOK 1 · Recall

What does bile do?

Reveal answer

Bile emulsifies fats in the small intestine, breaking large fat globules into small droplets that digestive enzymes can attack. Bile also carries waste products from the liver (bilirubin, excess cholesterol) for excretion in feces.

10 DOK 3 · Reason

Gallstones can block the cystic duct or common bile duct. Predict the clinical consequences of each blockage.

Reveal answer

Cystic duct blockage: bile cannot leave the gallbladder; the gallbladder distends and becomes inflamed (cholecystitis), producing right upper quadrant pain, especially after fatty meals. Common bile duct blockage: bile cannot reach the duodenum; bilirubin backs up into blood, producing jaundice. Pancreatic juice may also back up if the ampulla is blocked, causing pancreatitis. The location of the stone predicts the syndrome.

Pancreas 3 questions

11 DOK 1 · Recall

Where is the pancreas, and is it intra- or retroperitoneal?

Reveal answer

The pancreas is retroperitoneal (except the tail, which is intraperitoneal). It lies behind the stomach, with its head nestled in the curve of the duodenum and its tail extending to the spleen.

12 DOK 2 · Apply

The pancreas is both an exocrine and an endocrine gland. Describe each function and the structures that perform it.

Reveal answer

Exocrine pancreas (about 99 percent of the organ): clusters of acinar cells produce pancreatic juice — a mixture of digestive enzymes (proteases, lipase, amylase, nucleases) and bicarbonate-rich fluid. The juice drains through the pancreatic duct to the duodenum. Endocrine pancreas (about 1 percent): clusters called pancreatic islets (islets of Langerhans) scattered throughout the gland produce hormones (insulin, glucagon, somatostatin) that go directly into blood capillaries.

13 DOK 1 · Recall

How does pancreatic juice reach the duodenum?

Reveal answer

The main pancreatic duct runs the length of the gland and joins the common bile duct at the hepatopancreatic ampulla, which opens into the duodenum at the major duodenal papilla. An accessory pancreatic duct sometimes opens separately at a minor papilla just superior to the major one.
